My Journey into AI and Robotics

December 15, 2024
AI robotics personal

As a student at National Taiwan University, I've been fascinated by the convergence of artificial intelligence and robotics. This field represents the perfect intersection of theoretical computer science and practical engineering – a space where algorithms meet the physical world.

The Spark

My interest in AI and robotics began during high school when I first encountered the concept of machine learning. The idea that machines could learn and adapt, much like humans do, was both exciting and slightly intimidating. It raised profound questions about intelligence, consciousness, and the future of human-machine interaction.

Current Explorations

At university, I'm diving deep into several areas:

  • Computer Vision: Teaching machines to "see" and interpret visual information
  • Autonomous Systems: Developing robots that can navigate and make decisions independently
  • Human-Robot Interaction: Creating intuitive interfaces between humans and machines
  • Machine Learning Applications: Applying ML techniques to solve real-world problems

The Bigger Picture

What excites me most about this field is its potential to address some of humanity's greatest challenges. From healthcare robots that can assist elderly patients to autonomous systems that can explore dangerous environments, AI and robotics offer solutions that seemed impossible just decades ago.

I'm particularly interested in how these technologies might eventually help us explore space – imagine robots that can build habitats on Mars or AI systems that can make split-second decisions during interplanetary missions.

Learning and Growing

This journey is just beginning. Every day brings new papers to read, new algorithms to understand, and new problems to solve. The field moves incredibly fast, which means there's always something new to learn.

I'm grateful to be studying during such an exciting time in the development of these technologies. The next few years will likely see breakthrough applications that will change how we live and work.

Stay tuned for more updates on my projects and discoveries in this fascinating field!
